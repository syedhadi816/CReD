Question: Look at this set of 9 numbers: 32, 62, 64, 51, 35, 84, 12, 70, 40 How would the mean change if the number 50 were added to the set?
How is mean calculated?
Steps to Find the Mean:
	1	Add all the numbers together to get the sum.
	2	Count how many numbers are in the set.
	3	Divide the sum by the count.
What is the current mean?
Let's add all 9 numbers together:
32 + 62 + 64 + 51 + 35 + 84 + 12 + 70 + 40 = 450
Now divide by how many numbers there are (9):
450 ÷ 9 = 50
The current mean is 50.
 
What happens when we add 50 to the set?
Now we have 10 numbers. Let's add them up:
450 + 50 = 500
Divide by the new count (10):
500 ÷ 10 = 50
The new mean is still 50.
Conclusion:
The mean would not change — it stays at 50! This makes sense because we added a number that was exactly equal to the existing mean, so it didn't pull the average up or down.
