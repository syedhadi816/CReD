Question: Select the outlier in the data set. If the outlier were removed, would the mean increase or decrease?
2, 72, 74, 79, 83, 85, 93, 95, 99
What is an outlier?
An outlier is a number that is significantly different from the rest of the values in a data set — it stands out as unusually high or low compared to the others.
What is the outlier here?
Looking at the data set, every number is between 72 and 99 — except for one. The number 2 is far lower than all the others, making it the clear outlier.
What is the current mean?
Let's add all 9 numbers together:
2 + 72 + 74 + 79 + 83 + 85 + 93 + 95 + 99 = 682
Now divide by 9:
682 ÷ 9 = 75.8
What happens when we remove the outlier (2)?
Now we have 8 numbers. Let's add them up:
682 - 2 = 680
Divide by the new count (8):
680 ÷ 8 = 85
Conclusion:
If the outlier (2) were removed, the mean would increase from 75.8 to 85. This makes sense because the very low number 2 was dragging the average down — removing it pulls the mean back up toward where most of the data sits.
