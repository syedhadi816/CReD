Question: Look at this set of 10 numbers: 24, 73, 73, 67, 67, 98, 98, 57, 67, 45. How would the mode change if the number 73 replaced one of the 67's in the set?
What is the mode?
The mode is the number that appears most often in a data set.
Steps to Find the Mode:
	1	Arrange numbers from least to greatest to make them easier to count.
	2	Count occurrences — determine how many times each number appears.
	3	Identify the highest frequency — the number that appears most often is the mode.
What is the current mode?
Let's rearrange the numbers from least to greatest:
24, 45, 57, 67, 67, 67, 73, 73, 98, 98
Now let's count how many times each number appears:
	•	24 → appears 1 time
	•	45 → appears 1 time
	•	57 → appears 1 time
	•	67 → appears 3 times
	•	73 → appears 2 times
	•	98 → appears 2 times
The current mode is 67 — it appears the most.
What happens if 73 replaces one of the 67's?
The new data set would be:
24, 45, 57, 67, 67, 73, 73, 73, 98, 98
Now let's recount:
	•	67 → appears 2 times
	•	73 → appears 3 times
Now 73 appears the most, making it the new mode.
Conclusion:
The mode would increase from 67 to 73.
