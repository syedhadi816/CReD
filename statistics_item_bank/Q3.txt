Question: Look at this set of 5 numbers: 64, 43, 47, 93, 74 How would the median change if the number 90 were added to the set?
How is median calculated?
Steps to Find the Median:
	1	Order the data from least to greatest.
	2	Count how many numbers are in the set.
	3	Find the middle value. If there's an odd count, the median is the middle number. If there's an even count, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.
What is the current median?
Let's rearrange the 5 numbers from least to greatest:
43, 47, 64, 74, 93
Since there are 5 numbers (odd), the median is simply the middle value — the 3rd number:
The current median is 64.
What happens when we add 90 to the set?
Now we have 6 numbers. Let's rearrange:
43, 47, 64, 74, 90, 93
Since there are 6 numbers (even), we take the average of the two middle values — the 3rd and 4th numbers:
64 + 74 = 138
138 ÷ 2 = 69
The new median is 69.
Conclusion:
The median would increase from 64 to 69. This happened because adding 90 (a high number) shifted the middle of the data set upward.
