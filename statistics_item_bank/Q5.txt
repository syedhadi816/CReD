Question: There are 14 dogs at the dog park on a busy Saturday. 2 of them are springer spaniels. What is the probability that a randomly selected dog is a springer spaniel?
How is probability calculated?
Steps to Find Probability:
	1	Identify the favorable outcomes — how many of what you're looking for exist.
	2	Identify the total outcomes — how many total possibilities there are.
	3	Write it as a fraction: Probability = Favorable Outcomes ÷ Total Outcomes
Let's solve it!
Favorable outcomes (springer spaniels) = 2
Total outcomes (all dogs) = 14
So our fraction is:
P(springer spaniel) = 2/14
We can simplify by dividing both numbers by 2:
P(springer spaniel) = 1/7
Conclusion:
There is a 1 in 7 chance that a randomly selected dog is a springer spaniel.
